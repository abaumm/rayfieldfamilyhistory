About This Project

Rooted in the Ozarks,
documented for family


I've spent years piecing together the Rayfield family story — combing through census records, courthouse documents, old photographs, and the handwritten letters that somehow survived. This site is where I'm sharing what I've found.

It's built for the cousins I've met, the ones I haven't, and everyone who ever wondered where they came from. Everything here is sourced and cited — so you can trust what you're reading, and dig deeper if you want to.

If you have photos, letters, or memories to contribute, I'd love to hear from you.

Family Timeline

Full Timeline

1853

Arrival in Missouri

Elijah Rayfield and his family traveled from Kentucky to Crawford County, settling near the Meramec River.

1876

The Hughes–Rayfield Marriage

William Rayfield married Martha Ellen Hughes, uniting two of the region's most prominent farming families.

1917–1918

World War I

Two Rayfield cousins served in France with the American Expeditionary Forces. One did not return.

1944

World War II Service

Three Rayfield brothers served simultaneously in the European and Pacific theaters. All three came home.
