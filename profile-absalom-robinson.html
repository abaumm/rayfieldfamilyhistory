Absalom Parker Robinson

~1856–1858 – June 17, 1936
Des Arc, Iron County, Missouri
Compiled by Andrea Harter (née Rayfield), 2026 · Part of the Rayfield–Robinson Family History Project
Last updated: June 2026
⚠ Important Research Note: Two Absolom Robinsons There are two distinct, unrelated men named Absolom Robinson born within a year or two of each other. One was born and lived his entire life in Hawkins County, Tennessee. The other — our Absalom — was born in Missouri and lived there his entire adult life. Their records have been incorrectly merged on WikiTree, FamilySearch, Ancestry, and other genealogy sites. There is no evidence our Absalom was ever in Tennessee. This profile covers our Absolom — "Ab" — of Missouri. For the Tennessee Absolom ("Dock"), see the comparison section below.

Origins — What We Know and Don't Know

Key Finding (May 2026) Absalom Parker Robinson's biological parents remain unidentified. For years, genealogy sites listed his parents as John A Robinson and Mary Polly Manis of Hawkins County, Tennessee. This was an error. The child in that household was a different Absolom — see comparison below.

What family oral history tells us

In May 2026, Roxanna Robison — a descendant of Alexander Robinson — connected via the Iron County Missouri Genealogy Facebook group and shared the following, passed down through her father and uncle:

  • Absalom and Alexander Robinson were biological brothers
  • Roxanna also mentioned a Charles Arthur Robison — a half-brother to her dad, not to Absalom (a later generation)
  • Their biological parents died when they were young
  • The children were separated and sent to live with different family members
  • They were eventually raised as children of Isaac Robinson and Sarah Jane Goad
  • One brother later went to find the other

The "injured foot" reunion story

A family story has been told for generations: Absalom, as an adult, encountered another Robinson and asked him to remove his shoes. He was looking for a scar — an old injury to the foot, from childhood. The man had it. Absalom declared they were brothers. A military record has reportedly been found for a Robinson exempted from service due to an injured foot, lending the story a documentary shadow, though the specific record has not been identified.

Isaac Robinson — the guardian

Isaac Robinson appears in the 1860 Census in Arcadia, Iron County, Missouri, with a household that includes a young boy the census taker recorded as "Apsa" — age 5, birthplace Missouri. This is a plausible match for Absalom as a child in his guardian's household.

Isaac's 1850 Census (Sevier County, Tennessee) shows the same family earlier — with a newborn "Alexr" (Alexander) who is absent from the 1860 household. This aligns with the story of siblings being separated.

Open research leads

  • Roxanna says she has seen an earlier census showing Alexander and Absolom together "about 11 and the other younger" — before they appear with Isaac. That census could name their biological parents.
  • The "Parker" middle name could be a family surname — possibly a mother's maiden name. Worth searching Parker families in Iron/St. Francois/Madison County MO.
  • A 1918 Wayne County tax lawsuit names Alexander A. Robinson, A. Robinson, James Robinson, and Robert C. Robinson as co-owners of land in Wayne County — suggesting a shared inheritance and possible common parent.
  • Autosomal DNA testing between Andrea's family and Roxanna's family could confirm the Absolom-Alexander sibling connection.

Dock's family (for reference — NOT our line)

Dock was the son of John A Robinson (b. ~1822, Virginia) and Mary Polly Manis (b. ~1831, Hawkins Co, TN). He married Mollie Gonce on October 24, 1875 in Hawkins County and remained in Tennessee his entire life. His children: Robert, Victoria, Anthony, Otis, Lela, Mollie.
