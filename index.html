About This Project

Rooted in the Ozarks,
documented for family

I've spent years piecing together this story — combing through census records, courthouse documents, old photographs, and handwritten letters that somehow survived. This site is where I'm sharing what I've found.

It's built for the cousins I've met, the ones I haven't, and everyone who ever wondered where they came from. Everything here is sourced and cited — so you can trust what you're reading, and dig deeper if you want to.

If you have photos, letters, or memories to contribute, I'd love to hear from you.

The Rayfield Story

The narrative history,
chapter by chapter

I've written a full narrative history of the family — not just names and dates, but context: what was happening in Missouri, what drove people to move, why decisions were made. It reads like a story because it is one.

A note on the sources: family history rarely runs in a straight line. Where the records disagree — as they do, for example, on whether young Samuel Rayfield was born in Missouri or in Texas — I weigh each source by who was reporting and how close they stood to the event, and I say where the evidence lands. Everything here rests on primary records, cited throughout.

Buffington Community · Reynolds & Iron Counties

Six pioneer families, one lineage

In the 1830s, a cluster of families began building lives in the limestone hill country around Sinking Creek in Reynolds County — and over the following decades, they married so thoroughly into each other's lines that a single reunion at Buffington Cemetery in 1954 required a numbered photograph to tell who was who.

The surnames carried in this history — Rayfield, Mann, Farris, Seal, Robinson, Lewis — are not simply families that were researched. They are all in one lineage, connected through six generations of documented marriages in Reynolds and Iron Counties.

The Pioneer Families →
Rayfield & Mann Arrived Reynolds County 1829. The patriarch John Carter Rayfield married Polly Mann, whose family was already on Sinking Creek. Farris & Seal Lucian Farris in Reynolds County by 1850. Benjamin Seal born in Iron County, 1859. Their children married into the Rayfield family within two generations. Lewis & Robinson William Lewis and the Robinson family both in Iron County by 1859. Their lines converged when Katie Lewis married John Wesley Robinson in 1902.
